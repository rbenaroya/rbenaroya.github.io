Mount Everest

Everest has always been a dangerous place, and 2016 has just seen its deadliest week of the season. Five people have lost their lives and one still remains missing. A 25-year-old named Phurbu Sherpa fell while trying to fix a route just 150 meters below Everest's summit last Thursday. You will be climbing to the peak of Everest. The trip up is the easy part. Most die on the descent!

Risk

  • Life and limb.
  • Frost bite...don't be afraid to loose a finger or toe.
  • Avalanche!!! You might be buried alive.
  • Falling into a crevasse or the abyss

Costs

  • Training: $8000
    Training is a must if you want to step foot on one of the world's most deadliest mountains. You've got to be in shape, have stamina and strength to survive the climb.
  • Gear: $10,000
    Who knew 32 pounds of gear would be so expensive?
  • The Climb: $35,000-$100,000
    Permits start at $10,000, plus the cost of sherpas (porters, cooks, guides, etc.) is required by the government to get you to the top. Prices don't include air travel which can range from $1500-$3500. Don't for get to tip each Sherpa $500.

Gear List

  • Footware: climbing boots, gaiters, assorted weight wool socks, crampons
  • Clothing: Long underwear, synthetic jacket, insulated down suit, waterproof suit, GoreTex gloves, face mask, baclava, wool hat.
  • Accessories: Sunglasses, goggles, headlamp, batteries, cribbage board.
  • Climbing Equipment: Ice axe, crampons, harnes, carainers, webbing, cored, rope, aschenders, rappel device.
  • Camping Gear: Backpack, Sleeping bag, stuff sack, sleeping pad, water bottles, pack towels, trekking poles, stove, camp utensils, water filter.
